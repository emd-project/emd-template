## Introduction

This article is a **model**. It is a reference for writing: it shows the blog layout
(sticky sidebar + table of contents) and the editorial components available. The golden
rule: well-structured headings produce a good table of contents and good SEO automatically.

## How to read this page

### The table of contents (sidebar)

On the right (on large screens), the **sticky sidebar** shows the table of contents. It is
built automatically from the `##` (H2) and `###` (H3) headings. Nothing to do manually:
just write real headings, in order.

### The “Key takeaways” box

At the top of the article, the “Key takeaways” box reuses the `aiSummary` frontmatter field
(3 to 5 bullets). It is what readers — and search engines — read first.

## Pointing to a source or a tool

The EMD model is **MENTION, with no affiliation**: we never place a merchant link or a
purchase CTA. To guide the reader, only two options:

- a **neutral link** to the official source or the brand's page (the ranking and the
  comparator render it automatically from the `url`/`sourceUrl` field);
- a **link to an internal tool** — the [comparator](/comparer), the [quiz](/quiz) or the
  [ranking](/classement).

## Article images (1 cover + 2 reused)

**Image doctrine** (see `docs/IMAGES-WORKFLOW.md`): **only one generated image per article**,
the **cover** (frontmatter `featureImage`, file `[slug]-cover.webp`). The body then gets **2
reused images** from the category visuals already generated (no extra generation), placed at
~1/3 and ~2/3 via `<ArticleImage>`. Syntax (replace with real paths):

```mdx
<ArticleImage src="/images/categories/guides.webp" alt="Precise description" caption="Category illustration" />

<ArticleImage src="/images/blog/category-guides.webp" alt="Another precise description" />
```

Never reuse the current article's own cover; for variety, use a sibling article's cover from the
same category.

## Conclusion

Structure with headings, fill in `aiSummary` and `faq`, add a couple of well-placed callouts:
the engine does the rest (table of contents, sidebar, structured FAQ, tool CTA). To go further,
point readers to the [comparator](/comparer) or the [quiz](/quiz).
